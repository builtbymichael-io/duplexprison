Level Information

All levels are designed for different types of inmates. These levels are made for the safety of the inmates and the guards. Some levels are more dangerous than others, but we ensure that all inmates are treated equally.

The levels below can be found from level 1 being the most dangerous and level 3 being the least dangerous. If you are looking for a specific level, please click on the level name to be taken to that level.

Classification for each level:

Level 1:

  • Violent Offenders
  • Gang Leaders & Organized Crime Figures
  • Escape Risks or High-Profile Criminals

Level 2:

  • Repeat Offenders with Violent Tendencies
  • Drug Traffickers or Arms Dealers
  • Inmates with Disciplinary Issues

Level 3:

  • White Collar Criminals
  • Nonviolent Drug Offenders
  • Short-Term Sentences or First-Time Offenders

Level 1

Level 1 is reserved for Duplex Prison’s most dangerous and violent offenders, including individuals convicted of serious crimes such as homicide, armed assault, or major gang activity. These inmates pose a significant risk to others, requiring the highest level of supervision and containment within the facility.

Despite their past actions, Duplex Prison believes that even the most hardened individuals can change. Therefore, while security remains a top priority, the environment is carefully structured to support rehabilitation, reflection, and behavioral reform over time.

Dorm Layout and Security:

  • Level 1 inmates are housed in high-security dormitories, equipped with reinforced barriers, constant video surveillance, and on-site 24/7 guardians (trained correctional staff) who monitor and mentor the inmates.
  • Each dorm is designed for individual cells to reduce conflicts, with soundproofing and secure locking mechanisms.
  • Movement is strictly controlled. Inmates follow a precise daily schedule, and transitions between activities are heavily supervised to prevent contact with lower-level inmates.

Daily Routine and Freedom:

  • Level 1 inmates receive 4 hours of free time daily, carefully scheduled to occur separately from Levels 2 and 3 to avoid cross-level interaction.
  • During free time, inmates can exercise, read, engage in monitored therapy sessions, or participate in structured rehabilitation programs focused on emotional regulation, personal accountability, and reintegration preparation.
  • Privileges are earned, not given — consistent good behavior over time may lead to transfer to a lower security level where more freedom is granted.

Level 2

Level 2 houses inmates who have committed moderately serious offenses but do not pose a consistent threat to staff or fellow inmates. These individuals may have histories of non-lethal violence, organized theft, or drug-related crimes. They are considered capable of rehabilitation under structured guidance.

Dorm Layout and Security:

  • Level 2 inmates are granted more personal freedom than those in Level 1 but are still monitored under medium security protocols.
  • They are housed in medium-security dormitories, each staffed by a guardian on duty for 12 hours a day, 7 days a week.
  • Dorms are organized into communal living spaces with reinforced partitions and daily inspections to ensure safety and accountability.

Daily Routine and Freedom:

  • Inmates receive 8 hours of daily free time, strictly separated from Levels 1 and 3 to reduce potential conflict or influence.
  • All recreational or educational activities—such as workshops, outdoor time, or counseling—require pre-approved scheduling, with time limits enforced based on the nature of the activity.
  • This system reinforces structure and accountability, while still offering opportunities for growth and reform.

Level 3

Level 3 is designed for inmates who have committed minor, non-violent offenses, such as petty theft, fraud, or first-time drug possession. These individuals pose minimal risk and often show high potential for reintegration into society. Many are nearing the end of their sentences or are enrolled in transitional programs.

Dorm Layout and Security:

  • These inmates live in low-security dorms that prioritize comfort and communal cooperation.
  • Each dorm is monitored by a guardian for 6 days a week, with random wellness and conduct checks on off-duty days.
  • The dorms resemble college-style housing—open, less restrictive, and encouraging of peer interaction and responsibility.

Daily Routine and Freedom:

  • Level 3 inmates are granted 12 hours of free time daily, giving them the highest level of autonomy within the prison.
  • Unlike other levels, they do not need to request approval for most activities, although certain privileges (like technology access or off-site programs) may still be regulated.
  • Activities are self-managed within a time-bound schedule, teaching inmates how to independently balance responsibility with personal growth—a core pillar of the Duplex Prison rehabilitative model.

Extra Facilities

These facilities give more oppertunity for the prisoners to work on some skills, rehabilitation, and community work. There designed based on the level an inmate is in to ensure safety and proficiency. Prisoners get different oppertunity based on their sentence and the level they are in.

Food Court:

  • The Food Court serves as a central dining hub within Duplex Prison.
  • Level 1 and Level 2 inmates share designated meal times in a monitored, communal environment to promote social interaction and emotional regulation under strict supervision.
  • Level 3 inmates, due to their transitional status and lower risk profile, dine in a separate section of the Food Court equipped with higher security features — not for containment, but to maintain structure and discipline.
  • Nutritional balance and dietary needs are prioritized, with meal prep often supported by supervised inmate kitchen work programs.

Rehabilitation Building:

  • The heart of the prison's mission lies within the Rehabilitation Building — a multi-purpose space that includes classrooms, vocational workshops, and counseling rooms.
  • Inmates from all levels participate in structured education programs, including GED prep, trades training (like carpentry and coding), and group therapy sessions.
  • This facility represents Duplex Prison’s commitment to reintegration, equipping inmates with both life skills and emotional tools necessary for a productive life beyond incarceration.
  • Each program is customized per level, with Level 3 inmates receiving more independent assignments and access to advanced courses.

Visitation Center:

  • Family connection is crucial for inmate rehabilitation. The Visitation Center allows scheduled contact with approved visitors.
  • Level 1 and Level 2 inmates are allowed open-table visitation under direct surveillance, helping them maintain family bonds and motivation for reform.
  • Level 3 inmates, due to past behavioral risks or court-mandated rules, engage in secure, behind-glass communication, ensuring safety without eliminating meaningful interaction.
  • All visits are supervised by trained staff and scheduled to avoid contact between different levels.

Gym & Outdoor Track:

  • Physical health is integral to mental health — and the Gym provides that outlet.
  • Inmates have access to indoor exercise equipment and an outdoor running track, all within fenced, secured zones.
  • Scheduled usage ensures that Levels 1, 2, and 3 do not cross paths.
  • Fitness programs, some led by inmate mentors or certified instructors, focus on discipline, teamwork, and mental focus.
  • Consistent attendance and participation can earn inmates privileges or eligibility for wellness workshops held in conjunction with the Rehabilitation Building.
